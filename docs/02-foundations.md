# Understanding Neurodivergence — Foundations & Behavior

## Foundations
- Neurodivergence affects learning, communication, and sensory experience.
- Sensory differences: hypersensitivity or filtering of sights, sounds, smells, movement.
- Spectrum model: degrees of difference; metaphorical belt system.

## Behavior patterns
- Social "click" with similar people.
- Ability to find flaws in everything.
- Refined taste in art/food; strong preferences.
- Overthinking when information is incomplete — prefer "because" and depth-first explanations.
- Productivity and preference for solo work on large projects.
- Sensitivities (misophonia, kinesophonia), special interests, hyperfixation.
- Need for expected organization and resistance to surprises.
- Gullibility, isolation, intuition, different learning styles (need proof).
- Love of systems, lists, hierarchies; clustering with similar people.
- Attraction to intelligence; preference for meaningful, clear communication.

## Odd side effects
- Exceptional athletic capabilities reported anecdotally.
- Tendency toward hyperfixation on traits, atypical sexual expression.

## Odd phenomena
- Dunning–Kruger and imposter syndrome can coexist.
- High performers academically; interest breadth; social media/follower affinity.
- Gender differences in presentation; history in gifted programs.
